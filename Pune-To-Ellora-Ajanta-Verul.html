UNESCO recognised World Heritage sites Ajanta-Ellora are located in Aurangabad District, Maharashtra. These places are famous for their magnificent cave architecture and attract tourists from all over the world.

Experience One-day Cab/Taxi from Pune to Ajanta-Ellora

Ajanta: At this heritage site there are around 29 caves carved out of rocks. These Buddhist caves belong to the 2nd century, called chaitya Grihas (prayer halls). The beautiful murals and paintings depict Jataka tales and the life of Buddha, protected by the Archeological Survey of India.

Ellora: A group of 34 caves belonging to Hinduism, Jainism, and Buddhism constructed between the 6th to 10th centuries CE. Rameshwar Temple, Kailas Temple, and Dashavatara are important caves. Kailasa Temple is the largest single monolithic excavation in the world.

Grishneshwar Temple: One of the Jyotirlinga temples, situated just 1 km away from the Ellora cave complex. It is considered the last Jyotirlinga out of 12.
